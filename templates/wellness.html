Nutritional Dietary Strategy

Foods to EAT

Prioritize anti-inflammatory, low-glycemic, and fiber-rich foods:

  • Leafy Greens: Spinach, kale, Swiss chard
  • Berries: Blueberries, strawberries, raspberries
  • Whole Grains: Oats, quinoa, brown rice
  • Lentils and Chickpeas: High-fiber vegetable proteins
  • Fatty Fish: Salmon, sardines, mackerel, tuna
  • Flaxseeds and Chia Seeds: Rich in Omega-3 fats
  • Spices: Turmeric and cinnamon
  • Beverages: Spearmint tea and green tea
  • Healthy Fats: Avocado and cold-pressed olive oil
  • Nuts: Almonds, walnuts, brazil nuts

Foods to AVOID

Limit intake of processed sugars and simple carbohydrates:

  • Refined Sugar: Sweets, cakes, table sugar
  • White Flour: White bread, bakery goods, white pasta
  • Processed Foods: Packaged snacks, ready meals
  • Commercial Dairy: Milk and cheese (may worsen acne/inflammation)
  • Stimulants: Alcohol and excessive caffeine
  • Trans Fats: Fried foods, margarine, hydrogenated oils
  • Sugary Drinks: Sodas, packaged fruit juices
  • High-GI Foods: White rice, potatoes, instant noodles

Lifestyle & Therapeutic Modifications

1. Exercise Strategy

Perform 30-45 minutes of daily moderate activity. Regular walking, yoga, swimming, or strength training builds muscle mass to absorb glucose, managing insulin spikes.

2. Sleep Optimization

Aim for 7-8 hours of sound sleep nightly. Poor sleep schedules trigger cortisol releases, which directly degrade insulin pathways and raise fasting glucose levels.

3. Stress Management

Incorporate daily mindfulness, deep breathing exercises, or meditation. Chronic stress and cortisol elevations throw off the delicate LH/FSH hormone balance.

4. Target Supplements

Under professional medical advice, evaluate: Myo-Inositol, Vitamin D3, Magnesium, and Omega-3 fatty acids to improve insulin binding and cycle regularity.

5. Routine Medical Care

See a gynecologist or reproductive endocrinologist every 6 months to check pelvic health, lipid profiles, glucose tolerance panels, and check ovarian cyst development.

6. Cellular Hydration

Drink 2.5-3 litres of filtered water daily. Proper hydration supports cellular metabolic pathways, aids kidney filtration, and regulates natural energy levels.

7. Cycle Tracking

Use a dedicated cycle tracking application to monitor menstrual bleeding length, flow severity, pain index, and ovulation states, capturing crucial trends.

8. Eliminate Smoking

Avoid smoking and tobacco exposure completely. Tobacco increases systemic inflammation, compromises vascular health, and worsens hormonal imbalances.

9. Yoga & Stretching

Incorporate specific yoga poses such as the Butterfly pose (Baddha Konasana), Cobra pose (Bhujangasana), and Child's pose (Balasana) to stimulate blood circulation to pelvic organs.

General Health & Maintenance Guidelines

1. Balanced Diet

Focus on whole, unprocessed foods. Balance plates with lean proteins, healthy unsaturated fats, and complex carbohydrates to maintain steady insulin baselines.

2. Active Fitness

Participate in 150 minutes of moderate activity weekly. Cardiorespiratory and resistance activities build muscle tone, aiding metabolic pathways.

3. Routine Checks

Schedule annual gynecological visits. Consistent checkups evaluate thyroid baselines, lipid levels, and ovarian health before cycles exhibit delays.

4. Daily Hydration

Drink 2-3 litres of fresh water daily to support digestion, promote cell recovery, clear metabolic wastes, and keep energy levels active.

5. Stress Control

Practice regular stress-reduction techniques such as breathing exercises, slow walking in nature, or reading to control nervous hormones.

6. Sleep Quality

Ensure 7-9 hours of regular sleep nightly. Stable circadian rhythms reduce systemic stress and keep metabolic processes working smoothly.
